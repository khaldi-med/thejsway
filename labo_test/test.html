Introduction

The water cycle, also known as the hydrological cycle, is the continuous movement of water on, above, and below the surface of the Earth. It is a crucial process that involves the exchange of water between the atmosphere, land, and oceans.

Stages of the Water Cycle

Evaporation

Evaporation is the process by which water changes from a liquid to a gas or vapor. This stage is driven by the heat of the sun.

Condensation

Condensation occurs when water vapor cools and changes back into liquid droplets, forming clouds in the sky.

Precipitation

Precipitation happens when the water droplets in clouds become too heavy and fall to the ground as rain, snow, sleet, or hail.

Collection

During the collection stage, water gathers in bodies of water such as rivers, lakes, and oceans, where it can eventually evaporate again, continuing the cycle.

Did You Know?

The water cycle is powered by solar energy, and it plays a key role in regulating the Earth's climate!

Importance of the Water Cycle

The water cycle is essential for replenishing freshwater supplies, supporting ecosystems, and maintaining weather patterns. Without it, life on Earth would not be sustainable.

Conclusion

The water cycle is a continuous, dynamic process that sustains life on Earth by circulating water through the environment. Understanding its stages helps us appreciate the importance of water in our daily lives.
